Further Implications

Macroscopic definiteness arises from constraint-driven persistence rather than observation or intent. Stable outcomes persist because only certain continuations remain admissible once coupling and translation constraints are included.

Measurement is a translation step that reports which continuation remains admissible; it does not cause feasibility selection.

Disagreement about outcomes can arise from differing translation maps applied to the same underlying state, producing divergent labels without underlying contradiction.

Classical behavior emerges as the stable attractor of the same rule under dense coupling, with record-stable basins resistant to small perturbations.

Explicit treatment of feasibility, boundary margin, and coherence trend dissolves many apparent paradoxes by ruling out incompatible continuations. No special causal role is required for consciousness. Coupling strength, persistence, and translation suffice as minimal explanatory elements.
